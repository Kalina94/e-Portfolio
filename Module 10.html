My MSc Cyber Security Project

This module involves the development of a Computing Project in Cyber Security and the production of a Dissertation. The dissertation is an essential and integral component of the studies as it allows student to carry out an individual piece of in-depth research. The project will also provide the opportunity to utilise a systematic method for planning, developing, documenting and defending a research project. Student will be expected to develop an advanced level of understanding of his/her chosen subject area, including the legislative and ethical considerations for his/her work. Finally, student will develop a stand-alone artefact to the design specification that he/she created and that will meet the requirements that were identified during the initial analysis stages of the project.

Learning Outcomes

At the end of this module, I shall be able to:

  • Systematically identify research problem(s) within a suitable research topic involving computing.
  • Carry out, structure, and present effectively a focused critical and analytical reading and synthesis of accessed information on the topic.
  • Articulate the professional, legal, ethical and social issues related to the chosen topic and follow relevant professional codes of conduct.
  • Evaluate critically the research conducted and present the resulting artefact as a form of a defence of the research.

Artefacts

The continuous module assignment includes:

  1. Research proposal and Ethical Application Submission
  2. Dissertation Submission
  3. Artefact Presentation

Research proposal and Ethical Application Submission

×
In this assignment, I had to pull together the elements that I have created to date to form a project proposal. This provided me and my supervisors with an explanation of the work I have to do and where I intend to focus my research for the duration of my project.

My proposal should include:
  • An introduction to the problem and the motivation behind my project, including my research question.
  • The project aims and its supporting objectives.
  • A skeleton of my literature review, highlighting key sections and the underlying topics.
  • An overview of the research methods that I will use for data collection and analysis.
  • A breakdown of my project into work packages, including a description of the artefact(s) to be created and my timeline.
  • An indication of the CyBOK Category and associated Knowledge Area on which my research is based.
  • The key ethical considerations involved in my project, with particular attention as to how to ensure the safety of any participants.

The dissertation is the culmination of my studies at postgraduate level and is the finale to what will have been a long and challenging road to reach this stage.   • Introduction
  • Literature Review
  • Methodology

Reflections

Following this module, I have been able to:

  • Contextualise the role of your project and dissertation in your studies.
  • Understand the methods of project planning.
  • Investigate ways of identifying, quantifying and mitigating any risks.
  • Explore a range of project types and appropriate research methods.
  • Develop the concept of a literature review and horizon scanning.
  • Engage with the ethical issues associated with your research.
  • Understand the issues surrounding intellectual property in research.
  • Engage with the Cyber Security Body of Knowledge (CyBOK) to identify research topics.
The project has provided me the opportunity to utilise a systematic method for planning, developing, documenting and defending a research project. Additionally, with the help of my supervisors, I was able to tackle an independent, technically focused project of a chosen topic.

Consequently, the project allowed me to develop my technical skills, such as coding, algorithmic development, and data analysis, while also developing a range of other transferable skills, including:

✓ Critical thinking
✓ Planning
✓ Communication (both written and oral)
✓ Investigation
✓ Time management
✓ Data collection
