Information Risk Management

Underpinning concepts and principles of Information Risk Management (IRM) which includes a review of traditional and contemporary Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) models, focusing on the areas most affected by risk management considerations.

Learning Outcomes

At the end of this module, I shall be able to:

  • identify and analyse critically IT system risks and problems, and identify appropriate methodologies, tools and techniques to solve/mitigate them
  • evaluate and adapt programs and systems to produce a solution that meets the design brief
  • critically analyse and evaluate solutions produced
  • systematically develop and implement the skills required to be effective member of a development team in a virtual professional environment, adopting real-life perspectives on roles and team organisation

Feedback Obtained

Knowledge: A good assessment of the proposed 'challenge' - I particularly enjoyed the use of secondary sources to back up your assumptions. Good work in the forum posts as well.
Application of Knowledge: Your mixture of risks and assumptions demonstrates a good application of the subject material. I also appreciated the breakdown of the risks by option - another good application of knowledge
Criticality: The use of secondary sources demonstrates a critical approach to the material and challenge - good work.
Structure & presentation: I enjoyed the use of a tabular approach - a very good way to get a lot of information presented succinctly. However you still exceeded the word limit - which is a pity and were unfortunately penalised for it. You also failed to provide any form of timeline/ plan which was a written requirement and so lost all the marks for that section. There were also a few very obvious typos. Consider using more diagrams and illustrations which can help with keeping word count down.

Risk Assessment Report

×
Our team was expected to create a:
  1. Comprehensive risk assessment and report, recommending which solution the company should select
  2. Disaster Recovery solution for whichever option you recommend. they require a Recovery Point Objective of 15 minutes and a Recovery Time Objective of 4 hours
→ Risk Assessment Report

Feedback Obtained

You made a good effort in this report but it seemed somewhat rushed. It lacked sources/ background data/ research and really need some numbers to support your recommendations.
It was good that you provided a comparison and evaluation of risks but it was unclear what your recommendation was. Also your DR solution was well-argued but unfortunately, it did not meet the requirements provided in the assessment.
Consider using sources both to provide you with data as well as introducing alternative viewpoints to support your case.
This report still had several easily spotted and checked typos and errors. Consider employing a buddy system where you each check each others work.

Feedback Obtained

Knowledge and understanding of the topic/ issues under consideration: 40%
The code has an implementation of a solution to mitigate false login attacks. The solution is an implementation of a one-time password (OTPs) using a 2FA technology. The implementation employed Python, python-based Flask framework and Twilio API. The use of these technologies to secure user login details is well justified.
Application of knowledge and understanding: 30%
Even though documentation was clear to follow and the implementation logic was sound, the implementation did not ensure good password and username policies. For example, minimum length the format of acceptable passwords. These would have strengthened the security further and criticality of your assessment of the security and implementation strategy.
Structure and Presentation (as detailed in the assessment guidance): 30%
There is a very good documentation of functionality and lines of code. The README file is informative with information on tests carried out and the outputs from tests. You demonstrated some degree of criticality in your analysis and presentation of the proposed solution.
