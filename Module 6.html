The Human Factor

Humans are the inventors and designers of technologies that drive information systems and are also responsible for the security of these systems. The revolution of the internet and the ever-changing landscape of the Cyberworld are putting human behaviour at the core of matters related to Cyber Security.

Learning Outcomes

At the end of this module, I shall be able to:

  • Review the concepts and principles of human behaviour within the context of cyber security.
  • Design and critically appraise computer programs and systems to produce solutions that help manage and audit risk and security issues
  • Critically examine effective strategies to promote and manage the human factor in cyber security.
  • Critically evaluate the social and ethical considerations in developing and applying usable security.

Individual Presentation

The final assessment in this module, I had to present the solutions to the issues identified in the first assessment, including social and ethical considerations. The assignment required a recorded presentation of 10 minutes, along with a transcript of the audio file.

→ Presentation
→ Transcript

Feedback Obtained

Knowledge and understanding (merit)
You demonstrate some degree of knowledge and understanding of the human factors, highlighting various factors including flexibility in appointment scheduling, staff phishing emails and patients risk categorisation. While some of the issues identified and solutions proposed could help improve user experience, these are not in the context of cyber security except the staff phishing emails. Although, the Least Privilege Principle solution proposed and the justification around that could be considered. It is also unclear how the solution outlined would be evaluated and measured to ensure continuous improvement. A brief discussion around this would been excellent.

Criticality (merit)
You demonstrate some degree of criticality in discussing the measures and how these can be employed. For example, your point about 24% of doctors unable to spot phishing emails and relating that to the fact that doctors have the most privileged access to ASMIS which puts sensitive data at risk is very good. However, it would been excellent to also mention the patients in the discussion of the solutions as they are also users of ASMIS.

Use of relevant sources (merit)
There is evidence of wider reading and consultation of relevant sources as shown in thorough the presentation which is very good. Also, excellent use of sources and well referenced throughout the presentation.

Presentation and Communication Skills (merit)
Very good presentation and communication skills. Very clear message and good use of visual aids to aid the communication of the content. You employed good communication techniques to carry the audience along.

Structure and presentation (merit)
Very good structure and presentation. Slides are well easy to follow with good visuals. The beginning of the presentation gave a clear context for the audience.

Reflections

Following this module, I have been able to:

  • Develop an understanding of the security issues and risks associated with how people interact with computing systems
  • Develop the knowledge and skills required to manage and mitigate cyber risks in socio-technical systems
The module as a whole has equipped with the knowledge and skills required to be able to design usable security solutions. Also, the theories, concepts and principles from behavioural psychological and computer science to explore how effective usable security solutions can be designed, are explored in this module.

Professional skills matrix and action plan

This module has helped me to improve my presentation and communication skills, to the point that I can now express my ideas smoothly during a formal meeting with clients. It has also enhanced my technique of examining effective strategies for promoting and managing the human factor in cyber security, as well as the process of evaluating social and ethical concerns in creating and deploying useable security. I intend to improve my ability to manage and mitigate cyber risks in socio-technical systems, since this will undoubtedly benefit me in my future position.
