Secure Systems Architecture

TThis module introduces the underpinning concepts and principles of Secure Systems Architecture (SSA). This includes a review of the evolution of traditional operating systems architecture models, common security models, and the impact of distributed systems technologies on modern systems. The course provides a combination of an understanding of operating systems, distributed services and the security aspects of both.

Learning Outcomes

At the end of this module, I shall be able to:

  • Identify and critically analyse operating system risks and issues, and identify appropriate methodologies, tools and techniques to solve them.
  • Evaluate and adapt platforms and systems, using processes such as code refactoring, to produce secure distributed system solutions.
  • Critically analyse and evaluate solutions produced.
  • Systematically develop and implement the skills required to be effective member of a development team in a virtual professional environment, adopting real-life perspectives on team roles and organisation.

Development Team Project: Design Document

×
Our team was required to evaluate a provided scenario that models the activities associated with networked devices in a smart home (see Alexakis et al., 2019 and Cook et al., 2012 in the Unit reading). A smart home, in general terms, involves multiple IoT devices that are able to communicate with a centralised controller, and/or with each other. You are required to model the threats to and vulnerabilities of the system using Attack Trees.

Reflections

Following this module, I have been able to:

  • Develop the ability to contextualise the basic architecture of modern operating systems.
  • Understand of the relationship between distributed systems and operating systems.
  • Understand and apply efficient coding practices in the development of secure systems.
  • Understand the role of virtualisation in Secure Systems Architecture (SSA).
  • Appreciate current and future challenges, limitations and opportunities.
  • Reflect on and evaluate personal development.
